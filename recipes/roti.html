Roti Bread Recipe

Description

Roti is a round flatbread native to the Indian subcontinent made from stoneground whole wheat flour, traditionally known as gehu ka atta, and water that is combined into a dough. Roti is consumed in many countries worldwide. Its defining characteristic is that it is unleavened

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of wheat flour or durum wheat atta.
  • 1 cup lukewarm water.
  • 1 tsp. salt (optional)
  • 1-2 tsp. ghee (optional)

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, stir together the flour, salt, water and oil, until the mixture pulls away from the sides
  2. Knead until smooth and pliable, about 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat an unoiled skillet or tava to medium high heat.
  4. Divide dough into 12 equal parts, form into rounds and cover with a damp cloth.
  5. Flatten the balls with the palm of your hand, then use a rolling pin to roll out each piece into a 6 to 8 inch diameter round.
  6. Cook the roti for 1 minute before turning over, then turn again after another minute.
  7. The roti should have some darker brown spots when finished
  8. happy serving
